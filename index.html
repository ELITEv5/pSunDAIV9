How pSunDAI Works

What is pSunDAI?

pSunDAI is a lending protocol on PulseChain. You deposit PLS as collateral and borrow pSunDAI — a stable token worth ~$1 USD — against it. When you're done, repay the pSunDAI and get your PLS back.

There are no admins, no fees to a team, and no way to pause or change the rules. The protocol runs on its own forever.

Getting started — 3 steps

Step 1 — Deposit PLS. Go to the "Deposit PLS" tab (or use Auto Borrow to skip to Step 3). Your PLS becomes collateral locked in the vault.

Step 2 — Borrow pSunDAI. Use the "Borrow More" tab to mint pSunDAI. Each token is worth approximately $1. You must keep your Collateral Ratio above 150%.

Step 3 — Repay when ready. Repay your pSunDAI at any time using the "Repay" tab. Then withdraw your PLS. No time limit, no deadlines.

What is Collateral Ratio (CR)?

CR = (value of your PLS) ÷ (pSunDAI you owe) × 100%.

Example: You deposit $300 worth of PLS and borrow 150 pSunDAI. Your CR = 300 ÷ 150 × 100% = 200%. That's healthy.

  • 175%+ — Safe zone. Good buffer against price swings.
  • 150–175% — Acceptable, but top up if PLS drops.
  • 110–150% — At risk. You cannot borrow more. Add PLS or repay debt.
  • Below 110% — Liquidatable. The Stability Pool or a keeper can repay your debt and claim your PLS.

How to stay safe

  • Keep your CR above 175% at all times as a buffer. PLS is volatile.
  • Check your vault regularly, especially when PLS price is falling.
  • Use "Repay to 150% CR" button in the Repay tab if you get close to the danger zone.
  • If your CR turns red, act immediately — repay pSunDAI or deposit more PLS.
  • There are no warning emails. Only you are responsible for monitoring your vault.

What is liquidation?

If your CR falls below 110%, your vault becomes liquidatable. In V9, the Stability Pool absorbs this automatically if it's funded — otherwise a keeper repays your debt and claims your PLS plus a bonus (2–5%). Either way you lose your PLS collateral but your pSunDAI debt is cancelled.

Liquidation is not a punishment — it keeps the protocol solvent. But it means you lose your PLS, so avoid it.

What is the Stability Pool?

New in V9. Anyone can deposit pSunDAI into the Stability Pool — you don't need a vault of your own. Your deposit helps absorb liquidations directly, and in exchange you earn a continuous share of the protocol's 0.5% stability fee (even when nothing gets liquidated) plus a share of liquidation bonuses. See the Stability Pool panel on the main page.

Auto Borrow

The fastest way to start: enter a PLS amount in the Auto Borrow tab and click the button. It deposits your PLS and borrows pSunDAI at 155% CR in a single transaction.

Repay All & Exit

To fully close your position, go to the Repay tab and click "Repay All & Withdraw PLS". This repays all debt and returns your PLS in one transaction. Make sure you have enough pSunDAI in your wallet first.

What's new in V9 ›

V9 responds to an external security review of V8 that found two confirmed, exploitable flaws — not theoretical concerns — plus a structural gap, discovered before V8 ever held real value:

  • Fixed — free collateral extraction. V8's clearBadDebt() gave away 100% of a vault's collateral for free once it appeared underwater, gated on a manipulable spot price. V9 requires the caller to actually repay pSunDAI and pays out strictly pro-rata with no bonus — a caller can never receive more value than they paid.
  • Fixed — unbounded liquidation manipulation. V8's spot-liquidation arm used raw, unbounded spot price once activated. V9 clamps it to within 15% of TWAP no matter how far or how long spot is suppressed.
  • New — Stability Pool. Liquidations are now absorbed atomically by pre-deposited pSunDAI instead of depending on a keeper holding capital and reselling collateral into thin PulseChain liquidity.
  • New — real yield. The stability fee (and flash-mint fee) now mint directly into the Stability Pool as depositor yield instead of sitting in an inert counter with no beneficiary.
  • Hardened oracle. Minimum pool reserve raised 10x (calibrated against real observed PulseX depth), a 3-of-5 minimum valid pool requirement, longer spot-confirmation window, and system debt capacity cut from 20x to 5x real DEX depth.

V9 was deployed without a professional third-party audit. It passed a 27-test internal Foundry suite (including tests that specifically reproduce and confirm the fix for both V8 exploits) and multiple author review passes, but that is not a substitute for professional review. Use accordingly.

Technical details (oracle, Stability Pool, emergency functions) ›

Oracle: PulseX 5-Pool Liquidity-Weighted TWAP + Clamped Spot

  • TWAP (Conservative): Slow to fall (4h confirmation for >1% moves down), fast to rise (30min). Used for all safety checks. Pools vote proportional to their stable-side reserve depth.
  • Spot Warning: If the real-time PulseX price stays 5%+ below TWAP for 90 continuous minutes, liquidations switch to a clamped spot price (within 15% of TWAP). Flash crashes recover before then — real crashes don't.
  • Max Safe Debt: oracle.maxSafeDebt() — 5× the rolling 24h minimum of combined valid-pool reserves. This is what actually gates the effective debt ceiling and per-vault cap.

Stability Pool

  • Provide/Withdraw/Claim: deposit pSunDAI, withdraw your compounded balance any time, claim collateral gains independently.
  • Liquidation absorption: anyone can call liquidateFromStabilityPool(user) permissionlessly on an eligible vault — no capital required from the caller, who receives a small flat tip as gas compensation.
  • Full wipeout handling: if a liquidation exactly exhausts the pool, the internal accounting epoch rolls over cleanly — pre-wipeout depositors keep their claim to that final event's gain, new depositors after start fresh and isolated.

Emergency Functions

  • Emergency Unlock: Zero debt + last deposit >30 days → withdraw regardless of oracle state.
  • Emergency Repay / Withdraw: If oracle has been dead for 7+ days, repay and withdraw without price checks.

Stability Fee

0.5% APY accrues on your debt continuously. 1,000 pSunDAI debt grows by ~5 pSunDAI per year. This is why your "Debt" stat slowly increases over time. In V9, this fee mints directly into the Stability Pool as depositor yield whenever it has deposits, instead of an inert accounting counter.

Protocol Tools

  • Poke Oracle: Anyone can call this to advance the TWAP state machine. Rate-limited to once per 30 minutes.
  • Reconcile: Net stability fees collected against any bad debt on-chain.
  • Clear Bad Debt (V9, changed): Voluntarily unwind a fully underwater vault by repaying pSunDAI. Payout is strictly pro-rata — no bonus, no free collateral.
  • Settle Debt: Burn your own pSunDAI to cancel accumulated protocol bad debt.
